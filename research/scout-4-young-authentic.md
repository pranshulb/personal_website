## Common Themes Across Authentic Sites:

1. **Unpolished "About" sections** - "Mediocre developer", "wannabe cook", honest self-description
2. **Living documents** - Updated timestamps, evolving content, not frozen in time
3. **Webring participation** - Connected to indie web community
4. **Mix of polish and raw** - Room for both essays and rough notes
5. **Real problems** - Technical troubleshooting, life updates, genuine frustrations
6. **Strong opinions** - Not trying to appeal to everyone
7. **Non-monetized** - No ads, no courses, no "download my free guide"
8. **Hand-coded or minimal tech** - HTML/CSS, static sites, Neocities
9. **Personal infrastructure** - Self-hosting, custom domains, email addresses
10. **Badges, buttons, guestbooks** - Web 1.0 aesthetic revival

---

## Tech Stack Patterns:

- **Static site generators** - Jekyll, Hugo, custom builds
- **Neocities** - For complete beginners or aesthetic choice
- **GitHub Pages** - Free hosting, simple deployment
- **IndieWeb standards** - Microformats, h-cards, webrings
- **Minimal frameworks** - MkDocs, basic HTML/CSS
- **Self-hosted** - VPS, own infrastructure for the committed

---

## What to Avoid (Anti-patterns from this research):

- ❌ "Hi, I'm a Full Stack Developer" - Too corporate
- ❌ Perfect portfolio cases - Not authentic
- ❌ SEO-optimized blog titles - "10 Ways to..."
- ❌ Hiding personality - Professional persona only
- ❌ No rough edges - Everything polished
- ❌ Pure resume - No essays, no thoughts, just achievements
- ❌ WordPress defaults - Generic themes
- ❌ No updates - Frozen in 2019

---

## Key Insight:

**The best personal sites feel like walking into someone's workshop, not their showroom.**

They're messy, opinionated, evolving, and REAL. You get the sense the person would build this site even if nobody read it. That's the magic.
