# Digital Gardens & Personal Sites Research

**Scout Report: Philosophy Student / Community Builder / Writer Vibes**  
Date: March 13, 2026

This report covers 8 exceptional personal websites that exemplify digital gardens, networked thought, and thinking in public—sites that would inspire a philosophy student, community builder, and writer.

---

## 1. Andy Matuschak — notes.andymatuschak.org

**URL:** https://notes.andymatuschak.org

### Design & Layout
- **Unique nested note interface**: Notes slide in from the right as you click links, creating a horizontal cascade of thoughts
- Minimalist, text-focused design with no chrome or navigation chrome
- White background with black serif text (very readable, book-like)
- No traditional index or menu—you must follow links to explore

### What Makes It Special
- **Evergreen notes philosophy**: Each note is atomic, concept-focused, and evolves over time
- **Writes notes for himself first**: Explicitly states "They're roughly my thinking environment. I didn't write them for you!"
- **Work with the garage door up**: Shares work-in-progress thinking publicly as an experiment
- Complete transparency about the experimental nature of the project
- Deep integration with his research on tools for thought, spaced repetition, and learning systems
- Patreon-funded independent research model

### Tech Stack
- Custom-built system (not publicly available)
- States it's "still an early research environment" and refuses to make it available to others to avoid premature scaling
- Appears to be server-rendered with progressive enhancement

### Perfect For
Philosophy students who love interconnected ideas, people interested in learning theory, anyone who wants to see raw intellectual work-in-progress.

## 2. Maggie Appleton — maggieappleton.com

**URL:** https://maggieappleton.com

### Design & Layout
- **Visually stunning**: Clean, modern design with custom illustrations and visual metaphors
- Organized into clear sections: Garden (digital garden), Essays, Notes, Patterns, Library
- Beautiful typography with generous whitespace
- Custom illustrations and visual essays throughout
- Growth stages for notes: Seedlings → Budding → Evergreen

### What Makes It Special
- **Visual thinking**: Combines design, anthropology, and programming through illustrated essays
- **Definitive digital garden historian**: Wrote "A Brief History & Ethos of the Digital Garden" — THE canonical essay on the movement
- **Multi-modal learning**: Visual essays that teach complex topics through illustration
- Shows work from Github Next exploring AI and software engineering
- Emphasis on "imperfect notes, essays, and ideas growing slowly over time"

### Tech Stack
- Custom Next.js site
- Hosted on Vercel (likely)
- Uses MDX for visual essays with embedded React components
- Custom illustration pipeline

### Perfect For
Designers, visual thinkers, anyone interested in the intersection of anthropology/design/tech, people who want to understand digital gardening itself.

## 3. Jacky Zhao — jzhao.xyz

**URL:** https://jzhao.xyz

### Design & Layout
- Clean, minimal interface with sidebar navigation
- Organized sections: Posts (oak letter desk), Thoughts (workbench), Books (bookshelf)
- Graph visualization of connected notes
- Tag-based organization with bidirectional links
- Thoughtful use of metaphor (garden, desk, workbench, bookshelf)

### What Makes It Special
- **Creator of Quartz**: Built the static site generator that powers many digital gardens
- **Philosophy of agency**: Deeply cares about creating spaces that enable others to have agency
- **Agentic and communal technology**: Currently at Replit exploring how to democratize web tools
- Wrote "Networked Thought" essay defining rhizomatic note-taking
- "Hypertext garden" metaphor—explicit about the web as interconnected exploration
- Strong focus on play, questions without judgment, intrinsic motivation

### Tech Stack
- **Quartz** (his own creation) — fast, batteries-included static site generator
- Built on top of Obsidian/markdown files
- GitHub Pages or similar hosting
- Open source and highly extensible

### Perfect For
People who want to build their own digital garden, philosophy of technology enthusiasts, community builders interested in democratization.

## 4. Gwern Branwen — gwern.net

**URL:** https://gwern.net

### Design & Layout
- **Extremely information-dense**: Long-form academic-style essays
- Sophisticated popups/previews for links (hover to see summaries)
- Dark mode toggle, reader mode, search functionality
- Tag/status system: Shows completion level, certainty, importance for each piece
- Multi-column layout for sections
- Custom dropcap typography for essays
- Extensive use of sidenotes and margin notes

### What Makes It Special
- **30+ years of accumulated research**: Everything from AI scaling to darknet markets to anime neural networks
- **Meticulous scholarship**: Every claim sourced, every article evolving
- Unique meta-tags: Completion status ("in progress"), certainty levels, importance ratings
- **Long-term thinking**: Some essays evolve over decades
- Newsletter with 1000s of subscribers
- Philosophy: "This is a long content repository; be patient in exploring"
- Combines fiction, research, self-experimentation, statistics, philosophy

### Tech Stack
- Custom Hakyll (Haskell) static site generator
- Extensive custom JavaScript for popups, dark mode, reader mode
- Self-hosted with sophisticated caching
- One of the most technically sophisticated personal sites on the web

### Perfect For
Deep researchers, people who love footnotes and citations, anyone interested in long-form rigorous thinking, self-experimenters.

## Common Themes Across These Sites

1. **Living documents over timestamps**: Content evolves, isn't frozen in time
2. **Networked/rhizomatic thinking**: Ideas connect across topics, not just chronologically
3. **Work in progress is OK**: Show incomplete thinking, embrace imperfection
4. **Learning in public**: Share what you're learning as you learn it
5. **Personal knowledge management made visible**: External brain, publicly accessible
6. **Anti-performative**: Write for yourself first, audience second
7. **Long-term thinking**: Sites meant to last decades, not just capture momentary thoughts
8. **Intellectual generosity**: Freely share ideas, citations, reading lists

## Tech Stack Patterns

- **Static site generators dominate**: Quartz, Jekyll, Next.js, Hakyll, SvelteKit
- **Markdown-first**: Content separated from presentation
- **Progressive enhancement**: Works without JavaScript, better with it
- **Custom solutions common**: Many built their own tools (Andy, Gwern, Jacky)
- **Open source friendly**: Most share their code or templates
- **Self-hosted or GitHub Pages**: Independence valued over platforms

## Design Patterns

- **Minimal chrome**: Navigation secondary to content
- **Typography-first**: Readability paramount
- **Bidirectional links**: Notes link to each other
- **Graph visualizations**: Show connections between ideas
- **Status indicators**: Completion level, certainty, growth stage
- **No ads, no comments**: Focus on writing, not engagement metrics
- **Dark mode options**: Accessibility and preference respected
