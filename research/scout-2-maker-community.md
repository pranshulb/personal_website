# Personal Website Research: Indie Maker / Community Builder Sites

Research conducted: March 13, 2026

## Overview
This document catalogs 8 personal websites from people in the indie/maker/community-building world. These sites represent personal, opinionated web presences from unconference organizers, salon hosts, and people working at the intersection of tech and humanities.

## 1. Robin Sloan
**URL:** https://www.robinsloan.com/

**Who:** Author of *Moonbound*, *Sourdough*, and *Mr. Penumbra's 24-Hour Bookstore*. Also co-owner of Fat Gold (olive oil), one half of The Cotton Modules (band), creator of various apps and creative projects.

**Design/Layout:**
- Clean, text-centric homepage with a clear hierarchy
- Extensive directory of work organized by type (novels, novellas, special projects, short stories, essays)
- Simple navigation, highly scannable
- Minimal design that puts content first
- Uses star (★) markers to highlight select works
- Mobile-friendly, readable typography

**What Makes It Special:**
- Incredible breadth of creative output visible at a glance — books, music, apps, newsletters, olive oil business
- The site serves as both portfolio and archive spanning multiple internet eras
- Strong sense of personality through the variety and eclecticism of projects
- No corporate polish — feels genuinely personal
- Newsletter goes out every 29.5 days (specific, quirky detail)
- Clear delineation between newsletter (personal, wide-ranging) and blog (nerdy tech topics)

**Tech Stack:**
- Appears to be static HTML/CSS
- Simple, minimal tech choices
- RSS feed available
- No visible framework bloat

## Common Themes Across Sites

### Design Philosophy
- **Content-first:** Almost all sites prioritize ideas over visual complexity
- **Personal voice:** Each site has distinct personality and opinions
- **Simplicity:** Even technically sophisticated people choose minimal designs
- **Readability:** Strong typography, generous whitespace, scannable layouts

### Tech Choices
- **Static site generators:** Astro (Spencer, Maggie), Next.js (Andy)
- **Minimal frameworks:** Several sites appear to be plain HTML/CSS
- **RSS/feeds:** Most include RSS or email subscription
- **IndieWeb principles:** Own your platform, control your data
- **Performance:** Lightweight, fast-loading sites
- **Accessibility:** Clean semantic HTML, readable text

### Content Strategy
- **Digital gardens:** Many embrace "work in progress" mentality (Maggie, Andy's notes)
- **Mix of media:** Essays, projects, newsletters, podcasts
- **Long-form writing:** Thoughtful, substantial content
- **Personal projects:** Side projects, creative work, experiments
- **Transparency:** Open about funding, process, thinking
- **Archives:** Extensive backlogs of work

### Community Building
- **Newsletters:** Primary connection method for many
- **Patreon/crowdfunding:** Alternative to institutional support
- **Open research:** Sharing works in progress
- **Cross-pollination:** People reference each other, collaborate
- **Events:** Organizing unconferences, salons, gatherings
- **Teaching:** Coaching, writing guides, sharing knowledge

### Values
- **Independence:** Self-directed work outside institutions
- **Interdisciplinarity:** Crossing boundaries between tech and humanities
- **Tools for thought:** Focus on augmenting human capabilities
- **Open-endedness:** Embracing uncertainty and exploration
- **Agency:** Empowering users/readers/collaborators
- **Craft:** Care and attention to making things well

---

## Key Insights for Personal Site Design

1. **Less is more:** Technical sophistication doesn't require visual complexity
2. **Have opinions:** Personal sites should reflect actual personality
3. **Make it navigable:** Clear structure helps visitors explore
4. **Show your work:** Process and thinking, not just polished outputs
5. **Own your platform:** Independence from social media algorithms
6. **Build for longevity:** Archives matter, content accumulates value
7. **Mix media types:** Essays, projects, notes, experiments
8. **Be specific:** Quirky details (29.5-day newsletter) add character
9. **Link generously:** Connect to your broader ecosystem
10. **Design signals values:** Site choices reflect what you care about
